Central Brooklyn School Mapping Project

How to use

  1. Select a demographic from the menu in the top right corner of the map.
  2. Press play on the timeline at the bottom of the map and watch the changes, or use the arrow to skip from year to year.
  3. Click on a school to see more detailed information.

How to read

  • Shape: Each circle represents a public school in District 16.
  • Size: A school gets bigger and smaller as enrollment goes up and down.
  • Color: A school gets darker and lighter as the percentage of the selected demographic in that school goes up and down. Gray indicates missing data.
  • Year: Each year displayed on the timeline refers to the school year ending in that year. For example, "2016" refers to the 2015-2016 school year.

Background

  • District 16 was established by the Decentralization Law of 1969, but its current boundaries were drawn in 1973.
  • From 1970-2002, Community School Boards governed the 32 districts created by the law, supervised by a central Board of Education.
  • In 2002, the Community School Boards were abolished, the Board of Education was renamed the Panel for Educational Policy (PEP), and both the PEP and the new Department of Education operated under the direct control of the Mayor and the Chancellor.
